Going to High School

I was going into a school of over 200 kids in my grade, and I knew barley anyone. I didn't want to go to school, I would have done anything avoid going to my first day of freshman year. All that I wanted to do was avoid school, and lay in bed. I knew I couldn't, I knew I had two different options, 1. give up and just never go to school again, or 2. suck it up and put a smile on my face. I choose number two, considering I knew number one wasn't very likely to happen. First day of school came, and what did I do?? I walked into school with a smile on my face. Not only was I brave enough to walk through those daunting doors, but I also met friends that have lasted for a long time.

Opening up in Therapy

When I was struggling, the last thing that I wanted to do was open up to a person I never knew! I knew that I couldn't hndle my anxiety or my depression without some major help, but I also knew I did not want to tell someone I just met all my dark secrets. The thing was, therapuy is meant for you to open up, or else they can't help. Everytime that I would go to therapy, I would avoid talking about the real matters at hand. I felt that I would never open up, I felt like I was some freak in this world. After many, many, many therapy sessions, I forced myself to talk. I forced myself to tell my therapist about my constant anxiety attacks, and all my links to depresion that I had. I thought she would look at me funny, think I was a freak, or just drop me like everyone else. Guess what happened next? She didn't drop me! She helped me to control my thoughts, feelings, and aniety.

Pitching my Idea to my Peers

At my school, you are forced to take a half credit of a business course. This was no simple task though, this business course you had the ability to create a business. As the go getter that I am, I decided to be the CEO of my company, Summer Sweets. This journey of making a fake company came at no easy taks. We formed products, social media accouts for it, we took it internation, we created a mission and vision statement, and we also created a marketing campaign. At the end of this long semester, we had to pitch our business to our peers. This task was always a scary thing for me. I knew I was going to go up infront of my twenty plus peers, and a teacher I looked up, and I was suppose to provide an amanzing pitch. I was scared I'd be judged or made fun of if I stumbled on a word or forgot what I was saying. this task scared me. Of course, when the day finally came, I was so anxious and did not want to do it. When I got up to pitch, I took a big deep breathe, and I did it. I pitched my idea!
